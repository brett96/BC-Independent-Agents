Why not just use ChatGPT? The hidden risk of consumer AI

Pasting member info into ChatGPT isn't private — or compliant

Consumer AI tools are powerful, but they were never built to hold protected health information. The moment an agent drops a member's name, ID, or document into one, they've handed protected health information to a third party — with no agreement governing it, no audit trail, and no control over where it goes next.

No BAA — so it's a violation

OpenAI does not sign a Business Associate Agreement for consumer ChatGPT (Free, Plus, Team, or Business). Without one, entering PHI is an impermissible disclosure under HIPAA — and each instance can count as a separate violation.

Your inputs can train the model

On consumer tiers, what you type can be used to improve the vendor's models unless you've found and flipped the right opt-out. A member's details can quietly become training data you can't pull back.

It can surface publicly

In 2025, roughly 4,500 shared ChatGPT conversations were found indexed in Google, and an estimated 100,000 were scraped by third parties — sensitive content people never meant to publish, surfacing in plain search results.

The liability lands on you

A breach doesn't have to be your fault for the liability to attach. HIPAA penalties run up to roughly $2 million per violation category per year, on top of breach notice, remediation, lost member trust, and higher insurance costs.

This is exactly the gap BookCover closes

BookCover lets agents use the full power of Claude without ever exposing a member's identity. Identifying details are filtered out before anything reaches the model, and the platform operates under a Business Associate Agreement — so you get the AI, and your members keep their privacy.

Why we build on Claude

We tested the field — Claude won on what matters

Medical and insurance language is unforgiving: a plan rule, a formulary tier, or two similar-sounding benefits can mean very different things. We chose the engine that handles that nuance best — not the most familiar name.

Our own evaluation

The most accurate on real medical & insurance detail

We trialed the leading assistants — ChatGPT, Gemini, and others — against real plan documents, EOBs, formularies, and member questions. Across that material, Claude read the nuances most accurately and consistently, with fewer confident-but-wrong answers. For work where a wrong benefit answer has real consequences, that gap is the whole point — so Claude is the engine under BookCover.

Reads long, messy documents

Claude takes in multi-page plan summaries, EOBs, and commission statements in a single pass and pulls out exactly what matters — the paperwork agents actually deal with.

Your data doesn't train it

On the Anthropic API that powers BookCover, inputs and outputs aren't used to train models by default, logs are kept only briefly, and zero-retention is available — unlike consumer chatbots.

HIPAA-ready under a BAA

Anthropic offers a Business Associate Agreement for its API — the contractual foundation BookCover builds on, so the engine itself fits inside a compliant deployment.

Safety-first by design

Anthropic builds Claude with a safety-first approach and a tendency to flag uncertainty rather than bluff — exactly the temperament you want when answers go to real members about their coverage.
